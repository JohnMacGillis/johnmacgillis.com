The Lead Story

You are a correspondent in 1901. File the truth — by wire.

Chase the year's real stories, decode them in real Morse, and choose what the paper prints: the scoop, or the truth.

Read it on the App Store → Wire me at launch

The wire runs from Halifax to the Pacific, and down it runs the news. You have come to Toronto with a letter of introduction and a single ambition — to become a great reporter at The Globe. What you file, and what you refuse to, is up to you.

Queen Victoria is dying at Osborne House. A president will be shot in Buffalo. A wireless signal is about to cross the Atlantic for the first time. You will cover all of it — and learn to read the sounder yourself, dot by dot.

What the job asks of you

— i —

Read the sounder

Real International Morse, taught dot by dot. By the end you'll copy a cable off the wire yourself.

— ii —

Cover 1901, as it happened

The Queen's death, the McKinley shooting, Marconi's first signal — real events, on the real dates.

— iii —

The scoop or the truth

Every dispatch is a choice. Print fast and loose, or stand the story up — the desk is watching.

— iv —

Work the corkboard

Pin the leads, chase the thread, and find the story the powerful would rather you didn't.
